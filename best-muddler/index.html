A good muddler is the unsung hero of cocktails like mojitos, mint juleps, and old fashioneds. It gently bruises herbs and spices without shredding them, extracting flavor without bitterness. The key choices are material (wood vs. stainless steel), weight, and shape. Wood muddlers are traditional and softer on glassware, but can absorb odors and splinter over time. Stainless steel models are virtually indestructible, easy to clean, and often weighted for efficient muddling. When choosing a muddler, think about the depth of your mixing glass or shaker, the grip comfort, and whether you need a flat or toothed base. Flat bases are gentler for simple muddling; toothed bases break up tougher ingredients like fruit or ginger. Weight matters: heavier muddlers require less elbow grease, but can be tiring for small hands.
