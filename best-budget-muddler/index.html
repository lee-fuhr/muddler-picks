Quick answer

The OXO Good Grips Wooden Muddler is the best budget option for beginners thanks to its comfortable grip and gentle, effective crushing.

A good muddler is the difference between a muddled mess and a perfectly extracted mojito, julep, or caipirinha. For budget buyers, the choice usually comes down to wood versus metal: wooden heads are gentle on herbs and won’t scratch glassware, but they require hand-washing and can absorb odors over time. Metal heads are tougher, easier to clean, and great for crushing ice or ginger, but they risk bruising delicate mint if you’re too aggressive. Both picks here are and avoid the common pitfalls of cheap muddlers, flimsy handles, rough finishes, and heads that are too small to work efficiently. You want a head that’s broad enough to cover the bottom of a rocks glass and a handle that gives you solid leverage without slipping.
